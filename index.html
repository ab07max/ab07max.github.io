Nutrimate

• Imagine a college student who is consumed with their studies and does not have time to properly plan out a diet of foods that he or she enjoys eating but will not intervene with his or her’s daily nutrients and calories needed to either maintain, gain, or lose weight.

• This Android app, consisting of 5+ use cases, will be able to aide this student by providing them with the necessary components, gathered from the Nutrition database (having 50000+ instances) provided by U.S department of agriculture, needed to maintain a healthy diet while still being able to enjoy their favorite food produce.

• This app works by having the user input his or her body weight, height, and goal weight. The app will then evaluate the amount of calories needed to either be in a caloric deficit or surplus to meet the user’s goals.

• The user will then have access to meals that meet the user’s goals requirements and from then on the user will be able to rate these meals on a five-star rating scale in order to determine which meal would be more likely to be displayed during future use.

  • Timeline: November 2019 - December 2019
  • Github: Nutrimate
  • Technology Stack: Android, SQLite3, REST services

CoviCure

• As the pandemic is growing, patients are worried if they can get a bed space, PPE kits, Food, etc., One concern about the impact of COVID-19 on hospitals is ICU capacity. Real-time efficient allocation of beds, ICUs may save many more lives. At present data on real-time hospital bed availability is not accessible to public. It is difficult to find a hospital bed space and what gets even more challenging is the patient service requests to tend to. Every second counts!

• Similarly, as a hospital staff reducing human - human interaction for resource allocation/requests is necessary to maintain social distancing. We feel a mobile application with all these functionalities would highly help hassle free journey for both. So this app’s primary focus is hospital management during COVID.

• Self assessment, COVID-19 positive confirmation from a test center will help patients to act timely from their home. Then, this app reserves a bed even before they reach the hospital using location API. Once admitted, a requests for other necessities can be raised within the app so the hospital management can consolidate the requests and tend to them in the order of their priority. This keeps hospital management organized and helps the staff and patients to have their needs met with social distancing in practice as every request goes through the app only. A treatment without hassle can provide confidence to the user and a sense of trust for the community which will make the rest of 2020 easy for us.

  • Organization: Georgia State University
  • Timeline: August 2020 - December 2020
  • Devpost: CoviCure
  • Technology Stack: Android, Nodejs, Express, Microsoft Azure, SQL server

Safest Paths through corona affected regions

• The fear of being infected from coronavirus is creating a domino effect in the cancellation of plans. And because of the mandate quarantine, travelling has become a major problem.

• No one can tell that traveling during the coronavirus outbreak is entirely risk-free, regardless of the age, health, or travel destination. Unavoidable situations propel us to travel, and while we travel, we will have to take necessary measures not to get affected. Now more than ever, preparation is key.

• This project mainly focuses on an algorithm that can give safest path from source to destination for corona affected regions in the city map of Georgia with 150+ counties.

  • Technology Stack: Python, Tableau, Kaggle

Modeling and Simulation of Self-driven cars

• Simulation has long been an essential part of testing autonomous driving systems, but only recently has simulation been useful for building and training self-driving vehicles using playback sensor data that cars collect in the real world and verify that the vehicle's self-driving software handles tricky situations appropriately.

• One potential benefit of self-driving cars is that they could be safer than human drivers who are prone to distraction, fatigue, and emotional decisions that lead to mistakes. However, to ensure safety, autonomous vehicles must evaluate and respond to the driving environment without fail.

• This project presents a Devs-based pedestrian crossing simulation study for a self-driven car system. Two phenomena, pedestrian crossing, and collision detection control were simulated and studied.

• This work aims to study how the pedestrian and obstacle interference affects the control unit of self-driven cars. In this project, the atomic model of the entities in the self-driven system were discussed. It also shows that using System Entity Structure to describe a self-driven system, is convenient and easy to be modeled in a simulation environment such as DEVS Java.

  • Organization: Georgia State University
  • Timeline: October 2019 - December 2019
  • Github: Modeling and Simulation for Self-driven cars
  • Technology Stack: DEVS Java
